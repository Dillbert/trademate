This policy explains how we collect, use, and share personal data when you use the App. By using the App, you agree to this policy.

1. What we collect

Depending on how you use the App, we may process:

  • Account data: authentication identifiers from Sign in with Apple and/or Google (for example your user ID with the identity provider, and such profile details as those services share with us).
  • Training data you enter: workout sessions, exercises, templates, notes, timestamps, and related fields you save in the App.
  • Recovery / readiness data you enter: daily check-ins you choose to log.
  • Subscription status: handled via Apple’s In-App Purchase system and our subscription partner (RevenueCat) to determine whether your subscription or trial is active.
  • Technical data: basic device/app context needed to run the service (for example OS version, crash diagnostics if you enable them through your platform’s tools).

We do not require you to provide health data beyond what you voluntarily log for your own training records.

2. How we use your data

We use personal data to:

  • Provide, operate, and improve the App (sync, storage, backups, and support).
  • Authenticate you and secure your account.
  • Process subscriptions and trials in line with App Store rules.
  • Respond to your enquiries sent to the contact email above.
  • Comply with law and protect rights, safety, and integrity.

3. Legal bases (UK users)

If UK GDPR applies, we rely on one or more of: contract (providing the App you asked for), legitimate interests (securing and improving the service, fraud prevention), and consent where required (for example where consent is the appropriate basis for a specific optional feature).

4. Where data is stored and processors

We use third-party infrastructure that may process data on our behalf, including:

  • Google Firebase / Google Cloud (authentication and cloud database storage for synced content).
  • RevenueCat (subscription status and purchase validation).
  • Apple & Google (sign-in and payment processing for subscriptions through their platforms).

These providers may process data in the United Kingdom, the European Economic Area, the United States, or other countries where they operate, subject to their terms and safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses where applicable).

5. Retention

We retain your data while your account exists and as needed to provide the service. If you delete your account through the App where this feature is available, we aim to delete associated cloud content in line with the App’s behaviour at the time of deletion, subject to limited retention where law requires (for example tax or fraud records held by payment platforms, which we do not control).

6. Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only with service providers who help us run the App (listed above), when required by law, or to protect rights and safety.

7. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or port your data, and to object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at the email above. You may also lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) if you are in the UK.

8. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

9. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

10. International users

If you use the App outside the UK, your information may be processed in the UK or other countries where we or our processors operate.

11. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the updated version with a new effective date. Continued use of the App after changes means you accept the updated policy, except where law requires additional steps.
